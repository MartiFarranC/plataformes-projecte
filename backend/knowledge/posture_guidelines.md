# Posture Guidelines

Good posture helps prevent neck and back strain, especially during prolonged device use.

## Key Principles

- Keep your head aligned over your spine, not jutting forward.
- Sit with your back straight and shoulders relaxed.
- Maintain feet flat on the floor and knees at a 90° angle.
- Keep the screen at eye level or slightly below to avoid tilting your head down.
- Avoid leaning or slouching for long periods.

## When using a mobile device

- Hold the phone at chest or eye height instead of bending your neck down.
- Take short breaks every 20-30 minutes to move and stretch.
- Use a stable surface or stand when possible to reduce static loading.

## Common posture problems

- "Text neck": forward head posture caused by looking down at a screen.
- Rounded shoulders and collapsed upper back.
- Hips sliding forward in the chair, creating a swayback.
- Asymmetrical posture from carrying weight on one side.

## Recommended corrections

- Gently retract your chin and lengthen the back of your neck.
- Relax the shoulders and keep them away from your ears.
- Engage the core slightly to support the spine.
- Reset your posture often and use reminders if needed.
